# From a maze at 4 to a spiral at 10.
## Programming, logic and maths without a screen.

*[photo, large: assets/photos/01-spirale-tracee.jpg]*
No other screen-free robot can draw this spiral.

One robot replaces several sets. Younger children learn logic and work through mazes, older ones move on to angles, geometry and drawing. Grids and activities made for other robots work as they are, with nothing to relearn. One purchase for the whole of primary school.

### Nothing extra to buy
The robot runs on any gridded mat or sheet: the ones you already have, or ones you can quickly print or draw yourself. No proprietary accessories to buy. The step adjusts to any cell size, 15 cm by default. A method is included: 12 lessons, from the first program to angles and spirals, in the spirit of LOGO and Montessori. Everything is open in the documentation, viewable before you order. One kit suits a group of 2 to 4 children: the robot, the wooden console and the cardboard command tiles. Children build the program by hand, right on the console.
*[photo: assets/photos/02-robot-sur-quadrillage.jpg, the robot on a grid]*

### Learning from mistakes
The program stays on the console, in front of the child. If the robot goes the wrong way or the line comes out crooked, the pupil finds the faulty step themselves, changes part of the program and tries again. Children learn to fix their mistakes instead of fearing them. With the marker it is even clearer: the mistake shows up straight away on the paper. This matches the school curriculum: angles, shapes and a first step into robot programming.

### The robot speaks
It says every move and every command out loud, in 17 languages. Drive it onto a word card and it pronounces the word: about 90 of them, from "house" to "cinema". It sounds out the letters of the alphabet and plays musical notes. So one robot covers not just maths and coding, but the alphabet, first words and music.

### 2 levels, 1 robot
4-7 years (early primary): moves and turns, mazes, sequences, loops, debugging.
7-10 years (upper primary): precise distances and angles, arithmetic, geometry of shapes, drawing with a marker.

### Already in use
Terra Numerica, a French science centre (Sophia Antipolis, a project of CNRS, Inria and the Université Côte d'Azur), already uses PrimaSTEM in its workshops.
